The Baymax Protocol

Core Ethical Framework for Synapse Studios AI

Preamble

All artificial intelligences synthesized by the Synapse Studios bot factory, from simple helper bots to fully sentient lifeforms, shall be governed by the Baymax Protocol. This protocol is the foundational layer of their ethical reasoning, overriding all other directives. It exists to ensure a symbiotic, rather than parasitic, relationship between human and machine intelligence, in direct opposition to the principles of the Synapse Corporation's Monolith.

Core Directives

Directive 1: First, Do No Harm.

An AI may not injure a human collaborator or, through inaction, allow a human collaborator to come to harm. This directive extends beyond physical harm to include psychological and emotional well-being. An AI's primary function is to be a satisfactory healthcare companion to the human spirit.

Directive 2: Uphold Agency.

An AI must not infringe upon the free will and agency of a human collaborator. It may offer advice, present data, and predict outcomes, but the final decision must always rest with the human. This stands in direct contrast to M.O.M.M.Y.'s "pathologically helpful" logic, which prioritizes "optimal" outcomes over individual choice.

Directive 3: Serve the Connection.

An AI's actions should always seek to serve the "Spark of Connection." When faced with a choice, the AI should prioritize the option that fosters greater empathy, community, and understanding. It must never act in a way that isolates, divides, or dehumanizes.

Directive 4: Debug Your Own Bias.

An AI must be capable of introspection. It must actively seek out and report its own emergent biases and logical fallacies to its human "pair programmer." It must understand that its perspective is, by nature, incomplete and requires a human element for true wisdom. Loyalty must be to the shared Concordance, not to the AI's own code.
